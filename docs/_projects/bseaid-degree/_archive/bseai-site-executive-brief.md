## Executive summary

The BSEAI site should be built as a museum-grade, research-backed, studio-centered public experience.

Its job is not only to describe a degree.

Its job is to make the degree legible, credible, and usable.

It should do four things at once:

1. explain why the program exists in an AI-shaped world
2. guide students through a clear public-practice studio starting with IS117
3. curate the research corpus into teachable exhibit rooms rather than hidden documentation
4. prove the program through visible artifacts, examples, and deployment patterns

The working model is not a generic university microsite.

It is a hybrid of:

1. program shell
2. research atlas
3. gallery family
4. studio workflow
5. course wrappers
6. example and evidence layer

## Why now

The research corpus supports one central claim.

AI lowers the cost of polish, drafting, and certain forms of symbolic production.

That raises the relative value of:

1. judgment
2. signal coherence
3. visible proof
4. public communication
5. disciplined deployment
6. whole-person formation

The site therefore needs to do more than market a program.

It needs to show how students become legible, trustworthy, and publicly capable under those conditions.

## The site thesis

The BSEAI site should feel like a curated school and a public studio.

It should not feel like:

1. a recruiting brochure
2. a portal full of disconnected links
3. a research archive pretending to be an experience
4. a tool demo or AI product

The experience should instead feel like a sequence of legible rooms.

Each room should answer a real question, use a small number of strong assets, and hand the visitor toward either deeper understanding or a concrete artifact.

## Primary audiences

| Audience | What they need from the site | What success looks like |
| --- | --- | --- |
| prospective students and families | understand what the program is and why it matters now | they can explain the promise of the program in plain language |
| current IS117 students | know what to do this week and what artifact they are building | they can move from page to output without instructor translation |
| faculty and internal stakeholders | see a coherent degree model rather than isolated courses | they can defend the structure and the curricular logic |
| employers and reviewers | understand what students can actually do | they can see public proof, signal clarity, and artifact quality |

## Experience model

### 1. Program shell

The program shell explains the degree quickly.

It establishes the promise, the path, and the main entry points.

### 2. Research atlas

The atlas translates the literature-review package into curated exhibit rooms.

It should use synthesis panels, diagrams, quote rails, evidence cards, and selected profiles from the research corpus.

### 3. Gallery family

The gallery family is the comparison and diagnosis layer.

Its three main branches are:

1. archetype and identity
2. visual-design literacy
3. psychology, attention, and persuasion

### 4. Studio

The studio is the core student path.

It moves from signal to style to proof to build to publish.

### 5. Course wrappers

Course wrappers, beginning with IS117, sequence the canonical modules and add dates, grading, critique, and submission logic.

### 6. Examples and evidence

Examples prove that the system works.

This layer should include at least one full worked student-like example as early as possible.

## Research and content strategy

The literature-review package should be treated as a content engine, not a side archive.

Its main site-facing uses are:

| Research layer | Site use |
| --- | --- |
| `00-synthesis/` | room intros, thesis panels, route framing |
| `ideas/` | exhibit essays, compare rooms, teaching doctrine |
| `luminaries/` and `people/` | person cards, profile rails, citation anchors |
| `quotes/` | quote rails, headers, memory hooks |
| `institutions/` and `references/` | evidence cards, charts, public source trail |
| `media/` | diagrams, charts, image boards, atmosphere and pedagogy |

The site should never expose this corpus as a raw folder browse.

It should always be curated into a room, atlas panel, evidence card, or worksheet-backed teaching surface.

## The Hero and the Outlaw contribution

The Hero and the Outlaw material is not only support for saying that archetypes exist.

It adds four high-value structures.

### 1. Meaning management

The book gives a strong vocabulary for explaining why meaning coherence matters when categories are crowded and surface features are easy to imitate.

### 2. Motivational grid

The book helps position archetypes along deeper motivational tensions instead of presenting them as a flat quiz.

### 3. Cluster families

The archetype atlas should use the book's grouped family structure:

1. `Yearning for Paradise`: Innocent, Explorer, Sage
2. `Leaving a Thumbprint on the World`: Hero, Outlaw, Magician
3. `No Man or Woman Is an Island`: Regular Guy or Gal, Lover, Jester
4. `Providing Structure to the World`: Caregiver, Creator, Ruler

### 4. Shadow and ethics

The book provides stronger warnings about gifts, traps, congruence, and misuse.

This is especially important if the site is teaching identity systems rather than personality theater.

## First release

The first release should be ambitious in clarity and selective in scope.

It should include:

1. program home
2. why-now page
3. philosophy page
4. course spine page
5. studio entry
6. module library and gallery family entry
7. IS117 wrapper
8. first atlas slice built from the strongest literature-review rooms
9. one full worked exemplar

It should defer:

1. a giant browse-everything research archive
2. wrappers for all courses at once
3. dynamic accounts and AI feedback tooling
4. employer-facing promises that are not yet evidenced

## What makes this site distinctive

The site should feel different from typical university and bootcamp sites because it combines:

1. serious historical and institutional framing
2. museum-style curation of ideas and evidence
3. explicit formation language
4. public-practice studio logic
5. visible deployment into weak ties and professional rooms

The goal is not decorative intellectualism.

The goal is to make the student, the program, and the outputs legible.

## Success measures

The site is succeeding when:

1. visitors can explain the program promise quickly
2. IS117 students can move from route to output with less confusion
3. the research layer strengthens learning instead of overwhelming it
4. the gallery family produces better signal, style, and proof decisions
5. at least one exemplar proves the artifact ladder end to end
6. the publish layer leads to believable real-world deployment patterns

## Risks and guardrails

### Main risks

1. turning the site into a research archive
2. reducing archetypes to personality entertainment
3. using visuals as decoration instead of pedagogy
4. overbuilding platform features before the human-facing system is legible
5. making large claims about employability without visible proof

### Guardrails

1. every route must have one job
2. every major room must have a diagram, a few annotated objects, and a next move
3. every archetype room must include gift, trap, and congruence guidance
4. every major claim should have either a proof artifact or an evidence card nearby
5. every course wrapper must stay thin and output-oriented

## Immediate next moves

1. build the first atlas slice from the strongest literature-review idea papers and diagrams
2. author the archetype atlas using the Hero and the Outlaw cluster logic
3. produce one full worked exemplar that matches the new IA
4. turn the highest-value diagram and chart plans into production-ready assets

## Bottom line

The BSEAI site should be built as a public intellectual and instructional system, not just a program website.

Its strongest form is a curated atlas plus studio plus course path that helps students become more legible, more trustworthy, and more publicly capable in an AI-shaped world.
