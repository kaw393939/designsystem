## Working claim

This should not be framed as a generic AI survey degree.

It should be framed as a formation-and-deployment degree for AI-native builders.

Graduates should leave able to:

1. build LLM and agentic systems
2. explain them clearly
3. evaluate and govern them responsibly
4. deploy them into real organizational contexts
5. present themselves credibly in public

## Graduate role targets

Primary targets:

1. AI forward engineer
2. AI engineer
3. AI product engineer

Program specialization:

1. LLM application engineering
2. agentic orchestration systems
3. evaluation and operational discipline
4. enterprise deployment and adoption

## Student-facing frame

The student-facing story should be:

We have been here before.
History does not repeat exactly, but it often rhymes.

The Renaissance and Reformation were periods of changed cognitive and cultural leverage.
AI is creating a similar pressure today by lowering the cost of generating, adapting, and extending knowledge work.

That means students need more than tool familiarity.
They need the updated equivalents of the old formation disciplines: reading, writing, reasoning, measurement, systems thinking, and public action.

## Institution-facing frame

The program should argue that education now earns legitimacy by producing:

1. real builders
2. visible proof of competence
3. responsible deployment judgment
4. communication strong enough for cross-functional work
5. graduates who can adapt as the tool layer changes

## Program differentiators

1. strong technical direction around LLM and agentic systems
2. public legibility through portfolio and deployment practice
3. historically grounded framing instead of AI hype theater
4. explicit whole-person formation instead of assuming maturity and attention
5. leadership credibility grounded in high-scale teaching and active engineering practice

## Non-negotiable graduate capabilities

1. read technical and institutional context accurately
2. build working software and AI-enabled systems
3. evaluate outputs, risks, and failures rigorously
4. communicate to users, peers, and stakeholders
5. ship public artifacts and follow through in real networks
6. act with restraint, seriousness, and responsibility under uncertainty

## What the degree is not

1. not a prompt-engineering bootcamp
2. not a loose software degree with AI electives pasted on later
3. not a branding or influencer program
4. not a theory-heavy humanities critique with weak production expectations

## Internal positioning rule

The portfolio system should not sit beside the degree as a side project.
It should function as the public-practice spine that makes the rest of the degree visible and credible.
