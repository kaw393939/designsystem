# Faculty and Subject-Matter Review

## Status

- Lens: faculty lead or subject-matter owner
- Review target: seriousness, curricular intent, and fit with actual teaching goals

## Findings

1. The site now has the right seriousness level, but the amount of background framing still needs stronger rules so the course start does not become concept-heavy.
2. The formation layer is valuable, but it should consistently translate into concrete student actions or risk feeling ornamental.
3. The historical and philosophical rooms should be bounded by a doctrine that they exist to improve present-day judgment and artifact quality.

## Risks

1. Students may interpret the first weeks as theory-first rather than build-first.
2. Faculty may over-teach background material because it is well-curated and intellectually appealing.
3. The program could feel heavier than the first deliverables require.

## Required changes

1. Add a theory-to-action rule for atlas and philosophy use in course wrappers.
2. Add a week-1 and week-2 framing note limiting how much background is required before visible artifacts begin.
3. Add an instructor note that history and philosophy must always hand back to signal, proof, build, or publish work.
