# Accessibility and Inclusive Design Review

## Status

- Lens: accessibility and inclusive-design specialist
- Review target: IA, wrapper architecture, and media plan

## Findings

1. Baseline accessibility rules are present, but cognitive load rules for museum-style rooms are still missing.
2. Audio and transcript expectations exist, but they are not yet fully tied to room templates and asset packages.
3. Chart and diagram accessibility is implied, but long-description and data-summary requirements are not stated explicitly enough.

## Risks

1. The atlas could become overwhelming even if it is technically accessible.
2. Media accessibility may be handled inconsistently across routes.
3. Charts may be visually impressive but unusable for screen-reader or skim-first learners.

## Required changes

1. Add cognitive-load guardrails such as maximum panel counts, short-section preference, and summary-before-depth rules.
2. Add transcript and alternate-description requirements to the room and media package templates.
3. Add a chart accessibility standard requiring descriptive takeaway text and long description where needed.
