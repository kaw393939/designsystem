# Brand and Identity-Systems Review

## Status

- Lens: brand and identity-systems strategist
- Review target: archetype atlas, gallery family, and IS117 identity path

## Findings

1. The archetype atlas is much stronger after the Hero and the Outlaw integration, but it still lacks an explicit selection rubric that forces better decisions.
2. The IA includes gift and trap logic, but it does not yet give a compact congruence test for voice, visuals, proof, CTA, and actual conduct.
3. The deeper Hero and the Outlaw themes of category essence and organizational congruence are not yet translated into page-level or module-level checks.

## Risks

1. Students may still choose archetypes based on taste or flattery instead of signal fit.
2. Authors may teach archetypes as isolated language rather than as a full meaning system.
3. The public-facing identity work may become disconnected from the actual artifact or service being represented.

## Required changes

1. Add a primary-archetype decision rubric that weighs audience fit, proof fit, tone fit, and likely misuse.
2. Add a congruence checklist for voice, visuals, proof, CTA, and behavior.
3. Add a category-essence note that asks what the underlying page or artifact is promising beyond surface style.
