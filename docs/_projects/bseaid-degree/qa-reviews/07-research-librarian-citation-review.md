# Research Librarian and Citation Review

## Status

- Lens: research librarian and citation editor
- Review target: research corpus usage inside the IA and media plan

## Findings

1. The research corpus architecture is strong, but it does not yet define evidence tiers for public use.
2. The Hero and the Outlaw transcript cautions are known in source briefs, but those cautions are not yet elevated into IA-level provenance rules.
3. The system distinguishes charts from concept art in spirit, but the public-facing IA does not yet require clear labeling of synthetic concept assets versus source-backed evidence assets.

## Risks

1. Quotes or examples derived from transcript captures may be treated as publication-ready without verification.
2. Synthetic concept visuals may be mistaken for documentary evidence.
3. Authors may not know when a claim is safe for public summary versus when it needs deeper citation work.

## Required changes

1. Add evidence tiers such as `verified evidence`, `interpreted synthesis`, and `concept asset`.
2. Add quote-verification rules for audiobook transcript sources and other provisional materials.
3. Add labeling expectations that separate source-backed evidence cards from synthetic atmosphere or concept images.
