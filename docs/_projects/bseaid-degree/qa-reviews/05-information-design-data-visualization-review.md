# Information Design and Data Visualization Review

## Status

- Lens: information designer and data-visualization lead
- Review target: IA asset grammar and media production plan

## Findings

1. The asset-to-route map is clear, but asset specifications are still too high-level for production handoff.
2. The chart suites are prioritized, but the plan does not yet define a chart-card template with story question, caption, source block, and accessibility note.
3. The IA names comparison boards and diagrams, but board families are not prioritized by room.

## Risks

1. Production may generate attractive visuals without a consistent information hierarchy.
2. Chart outputs may vary in framing because there is no standard chart-card pattern.
3. Comparison boards may be produced opportunistically rather than according to room job.

## Required changes

1. Add an asset-spec model that includes format, job, source basis, required annotation, and QA owner.
2. Add a chart-card template for all evidence visuals.
3. Add a board inventory priority list tied to the archetype atlas, visual-design literacy, trust, build, and publish rooms.
