# UX and Student-Journey Review

## Status

- Lens: UX and student-journey reviewer
- Review target: learner-facing flow through the comprehensive IA and IS117 wrapper

## Findings

1. The route families are coherent for maintainers, but first-time students still need a more explicit `start here now` ladder at the program level.
2. The atlas is valuable, but its relationship to the core studio path must stay unmistakable or students may substitute browsing for building.
3. The IA clarifies support versus core path in IS117, but the same distinction is less explicit on the broader BSEAI home and route entries.

## Risks

1. First-time users may not know whether to begin in Atlas, Studio, or the course wrapper.
2. Atlas rooms may become intellectually appealing detours away from artifact production.
3. Program pages may feel richer than they feel directional.

## Required changes

1. Add a first-time visitor path with separate CTAs for prospective readers, current students, and instructors.
2. Add an atlas-framing note that explains when to browse and when to keep moving in Studio.
3. Add a clearer home-to-studio and home-to-course CTA ladder.
