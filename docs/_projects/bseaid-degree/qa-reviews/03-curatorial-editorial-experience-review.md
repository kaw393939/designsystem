# Curatorial and Editorial Experience Review

## Status

- Lens: curator and editorial experience director
- Review target: BSEAI IA with emphasis on atlas and gallery rooms

## Findings

1. The curatorial doctrine is strong, but there is no formal room template controlling how many panels, objects, quotes, or evidence cards a room should contain.
2. The IA says each room should feel curated, but it does not yet distinguish between hero objects and supporting objects.
3. Quote rails are named as a device, but their discipline is not defined, which creates risk of ornamental quotation rather than interpretive sharpening.

## Risks

1. Rooms may become overloaded because there is no explicit cap on interpretive elements.
2. All objects may be treated as equally important, weakening the museum-school feel.
3. Quote use may drift into mood-setting instead of meaning-making.

## Required changes

1. Add a room template that includes a recommended maximum for opening panels, annotated objects, quote rails, evidence cards, and next moves.
2. Add an object-selection rule distinguishing `hero object`, `supporting object`, and `reference object`.
3. Add quote-rail rules that define how many quotes belong in a room and what job each quote must do.
