# Influence and Judgment Luminaries

## Purpose

This file groups the main thinkers behind the project’s trust, persuasion, and judgment claims.

## Robert Cialdini

### Cialdini: why he matters here

Cialdini gives the project its most usable language for influence under uncertainty.

### Cialdini: what to keep

1. authority, social proof, reciprocity, commitment, liking, scarcity, and unity are practical influence levers
2. these levers work best when embedded in structure
3. influence should be tied to ethics and reality

### Cialdini: core sources

- [harperacademic.com](https://www.harperacademic.com/book/9780062937650/influence-new-and-expanded)
- [simonandschuster.com](https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/Pre-Suasion/Robert-Cialdini/9781501109799)
- [annualreviews.org](https://www.annualreviews.org/doi/10.1146/annurev.psych.55.090902.142015)

## Richard Petty, John Cacioppo, and Shelly Chaiken

### Petty, Cacioppo, and Chaiken: why they matter here

They explain why people sometimes process messages deeply and other times rely on cues.

### Petty, Cacioppo, and Chaiken: what to keep

1. message processing varies with attention and motivation
2. cues matter more when scrutiny is low or time is short
3. stronger arguments still matter when elaboration is high

### Petty, Cacioppo, and Chaiken: core sources

- [link.springer.com](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-1-4612-4964-1)
- [sciencedirect.com](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0065260108602142)
- [fbaum.unc.edu](https://fbaum.unc.edu/teaching/articles/jpsp-1980-Chaiken.pdf)

## Daniel Kahneman and Amos Tversky

### Kahneman and Tversky: why they matter here

Kahneman and Tversky provide the wider judgment-and-uncertainty background behind many persuasion claims.

### Kahneman and Tversky: what to keep

1. people use heuristics under uncertainty
2. framing changes judgment
3. decision quality is shaped by cognitive shortcuts, not only rational deliberation

### Kahneman and Tversky: what to handle carefully

1. do not use heuristics-and-biases language as a total explanation for all action
2. do not reduce ethics to “people are biased anyway”

### Kahneman and Tversky: core sources

- [psy.ucsd.edu](https://psy.ucsd.edu/~mckenzie/TverskyKahneman1974Science.pdf)
- [kahneman.scholar.princeton.edu](https://kahneman.scholar.princeton.edu/sites/g/files/toruqf3831/files/kahneman/files/prospect_theory.pdf)
- [psych.hanover.edu](https://psych.hanover.edu/classes/Cognition/Papers/tversky81.pdf)

## Cass Sunstein

### Sunstein: why he matters here

Sunstein helps define the line between guidance and manipulation.

### Sunstein: what to keep

1. choice architecture has ethical stakes
2. influence should remain transparent and accountable
3. behavior-shaping systems need justification, not only effectiveness

### Sunstein: core sources

- [cambridge.org](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/ethics-of-influence/E29EDE19EBCB53F6D8691730668115F7)
- [yalebooks.yale.edu](https://yalebooks.yale.edu/9780300262285/nudge/)

## Bottom line

Cialdini makes influence usable, Petty/Cacioppo/Chaiken explain processing depth, Kahneman and Tversky explain uncertainty and judgment shortcuts, and Sunstein helps keep the whole layer ethically bounded.
