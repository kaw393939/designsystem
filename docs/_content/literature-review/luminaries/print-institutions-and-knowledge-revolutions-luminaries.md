# Print, Institutions, and Knowledge Revolutions Luminaries

## Purpose

This file groups the figures most relevant to the project’s large historical and communication framing.

## Elizabeth Eisenstein

### Eisenstein: why she matters here

Eisenstein is central to the print analogy because she treats print as an engine of institutional and intellectual change rather than just a better copying method.

## Josef Müller-Brockmann, Brad Frost, and David Ogilvy

### Müller-Brockmann, Frost, and Ogilvy: why they matter here

This trio matters because the project is not only about institutional change. It is also about how order, systems, and evidence-led communication become legible in public artifacts.

### Müller-Brockmann, Frost, and Ogilvy: what to keep

1. Müller-Brockmann: grids as clarity infrastructure
2. Frost: systems and reusable components as modern constraint logic
3. Ogilvy: evidence-led, job-oriented communication where every part of the message must work

### Müller-Brockmann, Frost, and Ogilvy: core sources

- [niggli.ch](https://www.niggli.ch/produkt/grid-systems-in-graphic-design/)
- [atomicdesign.bradfrost.com](https://atomicdesign.bradfrost.com/table-of-contents/)
- [harperacademic.com](https://www.harperacademic.com/book/9780062937650/influence-new-and-expanded)
- [penguinrandomhouse.com](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/124131/ogilvy-on-advertising-by-david-ogilvy/9780394729039/)

## Bottom line

Eisenstein, Dittmar, Rubin, and Anderson help explain why knowledge systems change institutions. Müller-Brockmann, Frost, and Ogilvy help explain how clarity, structure, and persuasive communication should be built once that change is underway.
