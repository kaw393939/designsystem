# Motivation and Development Luminaries

## Purpose

This file groups the main figures behind the project’s motivation and growth claims.

## Abraham Maslow

### Maslow: why he matters here

Maslow gives the project its first usable vocabulary for need, deprivation, growth, and self-actualization.

### Maslow: what to keep

1. need matters before feature language
2. motivation is layered
3. higher growth motives matter
4. a person can be driven by multiple motives at once

### Maslow: what to handle carefully

1. do not teach the pyramid as a universal mechanical ladder
2. do not use self-actualization as a vague prestige word
3. do not ignore Maslow’s own caveats about exceptions, partial satisfaction, and multi-motivation

### Maslow: useful line

> “What a man can be, he must be.”

## Richard Ryan and Edward Deci

### Ryan and Deci: why they matter here

Ryan and Deci make the educational side of the project much stronger than Maslow alone can.

### Ryan and Deci: what to keep

1. autonomy, competence, and relatedness are central supports for motivation and well-being
2. social context can support or thwart development
3. high-quality motivation is different from controlled compliance

### Ryan and Deci: what to handle carefully

1. do not use “autonomy” to mean isolation or lack of structure
2. do not treat competence as self-esteem alone; it needs evidence and feedback
3. do not teach motivation as purely internal when the theory is explicitly social

### Ryan and Deci: useful line

> “SDT represents a broad framework for understanding why we do what we do.”

### Ryan and Deci: core sources

## David Kenrick

### Kenrick: why he matters here

Kenrick helps modernize the motivation layer by reworking Maslow into a more context-sensitive system.

### Kenrick: what to keep

1. motives are dynamic and life-stage sensitive
2. multiple systems can be active together
3. human behavior is not best explained by a single staircase

### Kenrick: what to handle carefully

1. do not use the evolutionary framing as a total explanation for culture or institutions
2. do not let the update erase the practical usefulness of need categories

## Bottom line

Maslow gives the project its need vocabulary. Ryan and Deci explain how motivation and growth are supported socially. Kenrick helps modernize the model so it stays flexible, contextual, and defensible.
