# Networks, Signaling, and Public Opportunity Luminaries

## Purpose

This file groups the key thinkers behind the project’s opportunity-formation logic.

## Michael Spence

### Spence: why he matters here

Spence explains why signals matter when direct information is incomplete.

### Spence: what to keep

1. markets rely on observable signals when deeper quality is hidden
2. educational and portfolio artifacts can function as signals
3. signal quality matters because people infer capability from limited information

### Spence: what to handle carefully

1. do not reduce human value to credential theater
2. do not treat any one signal as a full substitute for real capability

## Mark Granovetter

### Granovetter: why he matters here

Granovetter explains why weak ties matter for diffusion, mobility, and job information.

### Granovetter: what to keep

1. strong ties often circulate redundant information
2. weak ties bridge groups and carry novel opportunities
3. opportunity depends on circulation, not only possession of skill

### Granovetter: useful line

> “Stress is laid on the cohesive power of weak ties.”

### Granovetter: core sources

- [journals.uchicago.edu](https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/doi/10.1086/225469)

## Bottom line

Spence explains why the signal matters. Granovetter explains why public circulation matters. Together they justify the project’s insistence that a portfolio must be both legible and social.
