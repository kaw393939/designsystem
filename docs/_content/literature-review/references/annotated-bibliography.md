# Annotated Bibliography

## Purpose

This file collects the most reusable sources in the corpus and gives each one a short note about why it belongs.

## Motivation and development

- Maslow, A. H. (1943). *A Theory of Human Motivation*. Why it matters: the starting vocabulary for need, deficiency, growth, and self-actualization. Source: [psychclassics.yorku.ca](https://psychclassics.yorku.ca/Maslow/motivation.htm)
- Ryan, R. M., and Deci, E. L. (2000). *Self-Determination Theory and the Facilitation of Intrinsic Motivation, Social Development, and Well-Being*. Why it matters: the strongest foundational paper for autonomy, competence, and relatedness as a social-development frame. Source: [selfdeterminationtheory.org PDF](https://www.selfdeterminationtheory.org/SDT/documents/2000_RyanDeci_SDT.pdf)
- Kenrick, D. T., et al. (2010). *Renovating the Pyramid of Needs*. Why it matters: updates Maslow into a more flexible, context-sensitive model. Source: [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3161123/)
- Tay, L., and Diener, E. (2011). *Needs and Subjective Well-Being Around the World*. Why it matters: cross-national evidence that complicates simplistic pyramid claims while preserving the relevance of need satisfaction. Source: [doi.org](https://doi.org/10.1037/a0023779)

## Identity and archetypes

- Jung, C. G. *The Archetypes and the Collective Unconscious*. Why it matters: symbolic vocabulary for recurring narrative forms, with caution against treating archetypes as empirical personality labels. Source: [WorldCat](https://search.worldcat.org/title/The-archetypes-and-the-collective-unconscious/oclc/85401)
- Erikson, E. H. *Identity: Youth and Crisis*. Why it matters: the developmental backbone for identity formation beyond style alone. Source: [W. W. Norton](https://wwnorton.com/books/9780393311440)
- Marcia, J. E. (1966). *Development and Validation of Ego-Identity Status*. Why it matters: operationalizes identity development into statuses useful for analysis and teaching. Source: [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/5939604/)
- Mark, M., and Pearson, C. S. *The Hero and the Outlaw*. Why it matters: turns archetype logic into public communication and branding practice. Source: [WorldCat](https://search.worldcat.org/title/916062319)

## Attention, trust, and persuasion

- Lindgaard, G., et al. (2006). Why it matters: useful for first-impression timing and the speed of visual judgment. Source: [PDF](https://www.makinggood.ac.nz/media/1265/lindegaardetal_2006_attention.pdf)
- Petty, R. E., and Cacioppo, J. T. (1986). *Communication and Persuasion*. Why it matters: core text for elaboration likelihood and varying processing depth. Source: [Springer](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-1-4612-4964-1)
- Chaiken, S. (1980). Why it matters: supports heuristic processing claims that complement elaboration models. Source: [UNC PDF](https://fbaum.unc.edu/teaching/articles/jpsp-1980-Chaiken.pdf)
- Mayer, R. C., Davis, J. H., and Schoorman, F. D. (1995). Why it matters: strong trust-model source for ability, benevolence, and integrity claims. Source: [doi.org](https://doi.org/10.5465/AMR.1995.9508080335)
- Sunstein, C. R. *The Ethics of Influence*. Why it matters: useful ethical boundary text for persuasion and choice architecture. Source: [Cambridge](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/ethics-of-influence/E29EDE19EBCB53F6D8691730668115F7)

## Signaling, opportunity, and institutional change

- Spence, M. (1973). *Job Market Signaling*. Why it matters: formal foundation for why observable artifacts matter under uncertainty. Source: [Oxford Academic](https://academic.oup.com/qje/article-abstract/87/3/355/1909092)
- Granovetter, M. S. (1973). *The Strength of Weak Ties*. Why it matters: essential support for the public-circulation argument. Source: [University of Chicago Press](https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/doi/10.1086/225469)
- Eisenstein, E. L. *The Printing Press as an Agent of Change*. Why it matters: the strongest single source for the print analogy in the second-renaissance argument. Source: [Cambridge](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/printing-press-as-an-agent-of-change/7DC19878AB937940DE13075FE839BDBA)
- Dittmar, J. E. (2011). Why it matters: empirical support that print adoption had long-run economic effects. Source: [Oxford Academic](https://academic.oup.com/qje/article/126/3/1133/1855353)
- Anderson, B. *Imagined Communities*. Why it matters: helps connect media systems to public identity and shared discourse. Source: [Verso](https://www.versobooks.com/books/2259-imagined-communities)

## AI, labor, and student readiness

- Stanford HAI. *AI Index*. Why it matters: recurring baseline for adoption, capability, and system-level AI trends. Source: [Stanford HAI](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/)
- IMF. *Labor Market Exposure to AI*. Why it matters: one of the clearest cross-country treatments of labor exposure and distributional implications. Source: [IMF](https://www.imf.org/en/publications/wp/issues/2023/10/04/labor-market-exposure-to-ai-cross-country-differences-and-distributional-implications-539656)
- McKinsey. *The Economic Potential of Generative AI*. Why it matters: useful for function-level productivity and workflow change claims. Source: [McKinsey](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/mckinsey-digital/our-insights/the-economic-potential-of-generative-ai-the-next-productivity-frontier)
- NIST. *Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)*. Why it matters: anchors governance language in a public standard. Source: [NIST](https://www.nist.gov/publications/artificial-intelligence-risk-management-framework-ai-rmf-10)
- CDC. *Youth Risk Behavior Survey, United States, 2023*. Why it matters: hard evidence for whole-person student-support claims. Source: [CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/volumes/73/su/su7304a9.htm)
- Pew Research Center. *Teens, Social Media and Technology 2024*. Why it matters: current context for attention, device use, and ambient digital pressure. Source: [Pew](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2024/12/12/teens-social-media-and-technology-2024/)
