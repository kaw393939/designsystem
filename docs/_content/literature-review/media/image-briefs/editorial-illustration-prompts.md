# Editorial Illustration Prompts

## Purpose

These prompts are designed for concept art, visual exploration, and eventual website illustrations.

## Prompt 1: Second renaissance split scene

Goal:
Show the project's core analogy between print-era institutional change and AI-era knowledge production.

Prompt:
"Editorial illustration, split historical panorama, left side a fifteenth-century print workshop with presses, paper, type cases, and scholars, right side a modern AI studio classroom with laptops, diagram walls, model outputs, and collaborative student builders, warm paper textures, brass and ink tones meeting cool glass and screen light, sophisticated magazine-art composition, high detail, no text"

Recommended generation:
Landscape, high quality.

## Prompt 2: Signal to opportunity loop

Goal:
Visualize the identity-portfolio operating logic as an elegant conceptual system.

Prompt:
"Conceptual poster illustration showing need, signal, style, proof, call to action, publish, repeat as a circular design system, editorial graphic style, tactile paper overlays, directional arrows, fragments of interface mockups, portfolio artifacts, confident modern print-inspired composition, no labels, no text"

Recommended generation:
Square or landscape, high quality.

## Prompt 3: Trust and proof architecture

Goal:
Create a visual for the idea that trust comes from proximity between claim and evidence.

Prompt:
"Editorial systems illustration, layered cards showing claims, proof blocks, testimonials, metrics, project screenshots, and callouts arranged in a disciplined grid, Swiss-modern clarity with human warmth, cream, charcoal, rust, and steel-blue palette, refined magazine infographic mood, no text"

Recommended generation:
Landscape, high quality.

## Prompt 4: Weak-ties network field

Goal:
Give the project a visual metaphor for circulation, exposure, and bridged opportunity.

Prompt:
"Network illustration with clusters of people, dotted signal paths, portfolio artifacts moving across weak ties between communities, elegant editorial data-art style, subtle map-like geometry, warm neutrals with electric accents, serious and intelligent, no text"

Recommended generation:
Landscape, medium or high quality.
