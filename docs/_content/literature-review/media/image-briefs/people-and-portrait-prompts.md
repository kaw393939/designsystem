# People and Portrait Prompts

## Purpose

These prompts explore how people should appear across the future corpus and websites.

They are aimed at synthetic editorial portraits and student-builder scenes, not documentary historical likenesses.

## Prompt 1: Student builder triptych

Goal:
Create a people-first image for course marketing, landing pages, or lab introductions.

Prompt:
"Editorial triptych portrait of three early-career builders in a research studio, one writing, one arranging interface proofs on a wall, one reviewing diagrams on a laptop, diverse ages and backgrounds, expressive faces, natural posture, tactile environment with notes, printouts, and prototypes, cinematic but believable, no text"

Recommended generation:
Landscape, high quality.

## Prompt 2: Research mentor desk portrait

Goal:
Explore a visual language for mentorship, scholarship, and guided practice.

Prompt:
"Editorial portrait of a thoughtful mentor at a desk covered with books, paper notes, sketches, and interface artifacts, warm side lighting, intelligent atmosphere, not corporate, not fantasy, refined magazine photography style, no text"

Recommended generation:
Portrait, high quality.

## Prompt 3: Public-work studio scene

Goal:
Show public deployment as an active process instead of an abstract idea.

Prompt:
"Documentary-style editorial scene of a small cohort preparing public work, pinning up portfolio pages, rehearsing presentation copy, checking analytics and screenshots, layered environment of paper, screens, and conversation, sophisticated color grading, no text"

Recommended generation:
Landscape, high quality.
