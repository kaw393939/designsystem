## Key metrics

- **AI Occupational Exposure (AIOE)**: How much a job's core abilities overlap with current AI applications.
- **Complementarity (theta)**: How likely AI will augment rather than substitute the worker, based on context (responsibility, face-to-face, training).
- **Complementarity-adjusted exposure (C-AIOE)**: AIOE x (1 - theta - theta_min). Higher C-AIOE means higher substitution risk.

## Headline findings: advanced economies

| Finding | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Share of jobs in high-exposure category | approximately 60% |
| Breakdown | Some are high-complementarity (augmented), some are low-complementarity (substitution risk) |

## Occupation group classification

| Group | Likely category |
| --- | --- |
| Managers | High exposure, high complementarity (HEHC) |
| Professionals (legal, engineering, science, health, education) | High exposure, high complementarity (HEHC) |
| Technicians and associate professionals | High exposure, high complementarity (HEHC) |
| Clerical support workers | High exposure, low complementarity (HELC) |

## Notable occupation examples

| Occupation | Exposure | Complementarity | Interpretation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Judges and lawyers | High | High (theta high) | AI speeds research; final decisions remain human |
| Telemarketers and clerical support | High | Low (theta low) | Tasks readily automated |
| Oral and maxillofacial surgeons | Lower AIOE | Highest theta (approximately 0.78) | Very high complementarity but not highly exposed |

## Chart recommendation

Quadrant scatter plot with AIOE on x-axis and complementarity (theta) on y-axis. Label quadrants: HEHC (upper right), HELC (lower right), low-exposure (left half). Plot example occupations as labeled dots.
