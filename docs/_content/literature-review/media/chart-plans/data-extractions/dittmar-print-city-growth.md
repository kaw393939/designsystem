# Dittmar: Print Adoption and City Growth

## Source

Dittmar, Jeremiah E. "Information Technology and Economic Change: The Impact of The Printing Press." *Quarterly Journal of Economics* 126, no. 3 (2011): 1133-1172.

Source URL: <https://academic.oup.com/qje/article-abstract/126/3/1133/1854589>

Retrieval date: 2026-04-05

## Headline findings

| Method | Time period | Finding |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Difference-in-differences | 1500-1600 | Early-print cities grew approximately 21 percentage points more in population; mean city growth was approximately 30 pp, so early-print cities grew about 60% faster |
| Instrumental variables (distance to Mainz) | 1500-1600 | Estimated approximately 60 percentage point growth advantage for early-print cities |

## Context

- Growth is measured via city population as a proxy for economic dynamism.
- Study covers European cities with press adoption data from the late 1400s linked to population changes 1500-1600.
- Cities with presses established in the 1400s grew 60% faster between 1500 and 1600 than comparable cities without presses.
