# Student Readiness and Whole-Person Support

## Purpose

This file groups the institutional sources that support the project’s claims about reading readiness, mental health, digital saturation, and the need for whole-person educational design.

## ACT

### ACT: what it contributes

ACT provides a direct college-readiness benchmark source for the reading and communication challenge facing many incoming students.

Useful detail:

The 2025 national profile reports that 39% met the ACT Reading benchmark.

### ACT: core sources

- [act.org profile PDF](https://www.act.org/content/dam/act/unsecured/documents/2025-act-profile-report-us.pdf)
- [act.org report hub](https://www.act.org/content/act/en/research/pdfs/act-national-profile-report.html)

## NAEP

### NAEP: what it contributes

NAEP provides a high-level national baseline for reading performance.

Useful detail:

The 2019 grade 12 reading result shows 37% at or above Proficient, while the 2024 release shows 35% at or above Proficient.

### NAEP: core sources

- [nationsreportcard.gov 2019 press release PDF](https://www.nationsreportcard.gov/mathematics/supportive_files/2019NAEPgrade12MRpressrelease.pdf)
- [nationsreportcard.gov 2024 trends](https://www.nationsreportcard.gov/reports/reading/2024/g12/national-trends/)

## CDC

### CDC: what it contributes

The CDC YRBS material grounds the project’s mental-health claims in large-scale public-health evidence.

Useful line:

> “Overall, 39.7% of students experienced persistent feelings of sadness and hopelessness”

The same report also states that 20.4% seriously considered attempting suicide.

### CDC: core sources

- [cdc.gov article](https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/volumes/73/su/su7304a9.htm)
- [cdc.gov PDF](https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/volumes/73/su/pdfs/su7304a9-H.pdf)

## Pew Research Center

### Pew: what it contributes

Pew grounds the project’s attention and digital-saturation claims.

Useful line:

> “Nearly half of teens say they are online almost constantly”

The 2024 report also notes that 96% of teens use the internet daily.

### Pew: core sources

- [pewresearch.org article](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2024/12/12/teens-social-media-and-technology-2024/)
- [pewresearch.org report PDF](https://www.pewresearch.org/wp-content/uploads/sites/20/2024/12/PI_2024.12.12_Teens-Social-Media-Tech_REPORT.pdf)

## Why these sources matter together

These sources support a central design constraint for the larger project:

students are arriving in systems with uneven reading readiness, high digital saturation, and significant emotional strain. That means a serious AI-era course cannot assume attention, regulation, or confidence. It has to build them.

## Bottom line

The readiness problem is not an embarrassing side issue. It is one of the reasons the project’s whole-person, artifact-based, multimodal, support-oriented model is necessary.
