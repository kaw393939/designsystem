# AI, Labor, Governance, and Readiness Institutions

## Purpose

This file groups the institutional sources that support the project’s claims about AI-era labor shifts, governance, and educational legitimacy.

## Stanford HAI

### Stanford HAI: what it contributes

The AI Index is the best recurring source in this stack for broad directional evidence about AI labor demand, model progress, investment, and adoption.

Useful detail:

The 2025 AI Index chapter on the economy states that AI-related jobs in the United States rose from 1.4% of all postings in 2023 to 1.8% in 2024.

### Stanford HAI: core sources

- [hai.stanford.edu](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/)
- [hai.stanford.edu chapter PDF](https://hai.stanford.edu/assets/files/hai_ai-index-report-2025_chapter4_final.pdf)

## IMF

### IMF: what it contributes

The IMF provides macro framing around labor-market exposure to AI and preparedness for transition.

The project should use IMF material to justify institutional seriousness, not alarmism.

### IMF: core sources

- [imf.org working paper](https://www.imf.org/en/publications/wp/issues/2023/10/04/labor-market-exposure-to-ai-cross-country-differences-and-distributional-implications-539656)
- [imf.org blog overview](https://www.imf.org/en/blogs/articles/2024/01/14/ai-will-transform-the-global-economy-lets-make-sure-it-benefits-humanity)

## NIST

### NIST: what it contributes

NIST is the governance anchor for responsible AI in this package.

Useful line:

> “voluntary, rights-preserving, non-sector specific, and use-case agnostic”

That phrase is useful because it frames AI governance as practical and broadly adoptable.

### NIST: core sources

- [nist.gov](https://www.nist.gov/publications/artificial-intelligence-risk-management-framework-ai-rmf-10)
- [doi.org](https://doi.org/10.6028/NIST.AI.100-1)

## NACE

### NACE: what it contributes

NACE is the most directly useful source here for entry-level employer hiring behavior.

Useful detail:

The 2025 spring update reports that almost two-thirds of employers use skills-based hiring to identify job candidates.

### NACE: core sources

- [naceweb.org article](https://www.naceweb.org/job-market/trends-and-predictions/almost-two-thirds-of-employers-use-skills-based-hiring-to-help-identify-job-candidates)
- [naceweb.org report PDF](https://naceweb.org/docs/default-source/default-document-library/2025/publication/research-report/2025-nace-job-outlook-jan-2025.pdf)

## Bottom line

These institutions support the claim that AI is changing work, that governance matters, and that educational programs will be judged increasingly by demonstrable competence and readiness rather than prestige alone.
