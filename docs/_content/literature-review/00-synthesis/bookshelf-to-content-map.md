# Bookshelf to Content Map

## Purpose

This file turns the current bookshelf into a content-planning tool.

The point is not only to collect books. The point is to make the shelf produce stronger pages, modules, explanations, and visual systems for the project.

## Working rule

Every book on the working shelf should answer at least one of these questions:

1. How does a person become more legible, trustworthy, and useful in public?
2. What kind of character or discipline becomes more valuable when AI cheapens surface output?
3. What happens to institutions when access to knowledge or skill changes dramatically?
4. How should pages, proofs, and learning systems be designed under those conditions?

If a book does not help answer one of those questions, it should not drive the current content roadmap.

## Content cluster 1: Identity, signal, and archetypal coherence

### Main books

- `The Hero and the Outlaw`
- `Positioning`
- `The 22 Immutable Laws of Branding`
- `The 22 Immutable Laws of Marketing`
- `Pre-Suasion`
- `Blink`

### Core claim this cluster supports

People do not read portfolios or public-facing work as neutral information. They read for signal, category, trust, meaning, and interpretive coherence.

### Strong content outputs

1. a page on why polished pages still fail when the signal is mixed
2. a page on category placement and first-read meaning
3. a page on why proof has to sit near the claim
4. a page on ethical attention capture versus manipulative attention capture
5. a module on archetypal coherence in voice, imagery, and proof

### Best use inside the project

- identity portfolio lessons
- route-level signal and proof pages
- archetype visual maps
- page-review rubrics for first-screen legibility

### Best use inside the project

- whole-person curriculum pages
- support routes for confidence, discipline, and public action
- leadership and coaching sections of the AI-degree argument
- quote bank and editorial framing lines

## Content cluster 3: Renaissance, Reformation, and institutional change

### Main books

- `Thomas More - Utopia`
- `Erasmus - In Praise of Folly`
- `Martin Luther - Concerning Christian Liberty`
- `Martin Luther - 95 Theses`
- `Francis Bacon - The Advancement of Learning`
- `Life 3.0`
- `The Second Machine Age`
- `Dawn of the New Everything`
- `The Innovator's Dilemma`
- `The Innovators`

### Core claim this cluster supports

The AI comparison becomes meaningful when it is framed as a change in the economics of symbolic production and therefore a change in legitimacy, interpretation, learning, and institutional design.

### Strong content outputs

1. a page on print and AI as authority shocks
2. a page on why institutions lose credibility when they only gatekeep access
3. a lesson on conscience, method, and educational legitimacy
4. a timeline contrasting Renaissance and Reformation shifts with AI-era shifts
5. a module on why education must move from content delivery to formation and public capability

### Best use inside the project

- second-renaissance experience pages
- enterprise-AI degree framing
- charts, timelines, and comparison graphics
- governance and educational philosophy sections

## Content cluster 4: Design, method, and system building

### Main books

- `Change by Design`
- `Steve Jobs`
- `The Innovators`
- `The Innovator's Dilemma`

### Core claim this cluster supports

Useful systems are not built from information alone. They require intentional form, problem framing, standards, iteration, and coherent experience design.

### Strong content outputs

1. a page on why educational systems need designed scaffolds
2. a lesson on turning theory into repeatable page and workflow structures
3. a page on standards, craft, and the difference between coherence and ornament

### Best use inside the project

- educational design system pages
- process and recipe routes
- teaching notes for maintainers and authors

## What not to let happen

1. Do not let the shelf drift back into generic business reading.
2. Do not let classical texts become decorative prestige objects with no curricular use.
3. Do not treat self-development books as substitutes for character, method, and public proof.
4. Do not build AI comparison pages that skip the institutional and moral stakes.

## Bottom line

The bookshelf is now strongest when read as one coordinated research system:

- identity and trust for public legibility
- character and self-command for whole-person formation
- Renaissance and Reformation texts for the authority-shift analogy
- design and innovation books for turning theory into structure

That is the combination most likely to produce content that feels serious, historically grounded, and useful in an AI-shaped world.
