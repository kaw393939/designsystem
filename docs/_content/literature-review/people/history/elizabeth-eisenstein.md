# Elizabeth Eisenstein

## Why she matters here

Eisenstein is central to the print analogy because she treats print as an engine of institutional and intellectual change, not just a better copying method.

She matters whenever the project makes its strongest version of the second-renaissance argument.

## What this project should take

1. Media systems reshape institutions.
2. Lower-cost symbolic reproduction changes authority structures.
3. Knowledge tools can transform cultural and educational legitimacy.

## What to handle carefully

1. Do not force a one-to-one analogy between print and AI.
2. Use Eisenstein as a framing source, then pair her with contemporary evidence.
