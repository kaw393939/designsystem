# Shelly Chaiken

## Why she matters here

Chaiken is the key bridge from deeper elaboration models to heuristic processing under low attention or low motivation.

She matters because the project needs to understand what happens when users skim, scan, and make quick judgments.

## What this project should take

1. People often use heuristics when evaluating messages.
2. Cues matter more when scrutiny is low.
3. Fast evaluation is still structured, not random.

## What to handle carefully

1. Do not treat heuristics as proof that users are irrational.
2. Keep heuristic processing paired with ethics and real evidence.
