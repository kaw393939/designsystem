# Round 2 Site Think-Aloud

## Method

I reread the updated page set as a college-age student who is trying to understand what this site helps me do, why it is worth my time, and where I should move next. This round assumes the new face-led visuals, before-and-after comparisons, and story-led intros are in place.

## Global Read

- The site feels substantially more inhabited than Round 1. Faces, scenes, and progress visuals make the routes feel like pages for people rather than internal system notes.
- The identity course now has a much stronger emotional entry point. I can picture the learner and the stakes before I hit the theory.
- The guide pages are still technical, but they now translate that technical layer into a student-facing question: what does this change in the first read?
- The examples feel more believable because the placeholder gradients are gone.
- The remaining weakness is navigation energy. A few guide pages explain the examples well, but I still want stronger direct bridges from system-doc pages into the most relevant live routes.

## Page Notes

### `/` and `/experiences/identity-portfolio/`

- What improved: the page now opens with a human scene, visible portfolio contrast, and student portraits that make the system feel lived instead of abstract.
- What still feels weak: the page is much stronger, but the rest of the documentation layer still needs to keep pointing back to this level of concreteness.
- Round 2 move: keep guide pages tied to live route examples.

### `/recipes/`

- What improved: the route now explains why recipes matter to a learner, not just to the validator.
- What still feels weak: it works best when the exemplars are easy to jump into.
- Round 2 move: keep pushing direct route entry from adjacent guide pages.

### `/tokens/`

- What improved: the token layer finally reads as emotional infrastructure instead of palette bookkeeping.
- What still feels weak: the page previews are better, but they should be even more obviously clickable bridges into the live example routes.
- Round 2 move: add direct example-route actions to the preview cards.

### `/layouts/`

- What improved: the layout page now frames shells and media in terms of student attention, pacing, and trust.
- What still feels weak: the route already links to example pages, so the main remaining task is consistency with other guide pages.
- Round 2 move: keep cross-page route bridges consistent.

### `/process/`

- What improved: this now reads less like procedure for its own sake and more like a trust framework.
- What still feels weak: it is still a process-heavy page by nature, so it benefits from clear downstream pointers.
- Round 2 move: preserve the action paths already present.

### `/status/`

- What improved: the honesty of the page is clearer and more usable.
- What still feels weak: after I understand the status, I want one obvious way back into the most important live routes.
- Round 2 move: add direct “go see it” actions.

### `/primitives/`

- What improved: the primitive page now explains why reusable pedagogy matters in felt terms, not just schema terms.
- What still feels weak: the units already do a lot of the heavy lifting, so the main issue is just keeping navigation momentum strong.
- Round 2 move: leave the content focus on the rendered units.

### `/examples/module/`, `/examples/lesson/`, `/examples/reading-map/`

- What improved: these routes no longer look like scaffold placeholders. They now feel like actual educational pages.
- What still feels weak: they are strongest when guide pages launch me into them directly.
- Round 2 move: make the preview layer more route-forward.

### `/recipes/feedback-loops/` and `/recipes/public-space-observation/`

- What improved: the shared recipe wrapper gives these routes a real opening frame and visible movement.
- What still feels weak: no major issue beyond keeping the surrounding guide pages connected to them.
- Round 2 move: no local change required if route bridges improve elsewhere.

### `/experiences/ai-second-renaissance/`

- What improved: the route now opens with history, labor pressure, and signal rather than a bare unit render.
- What still feels weak: it remains a quieter experience than identity, which is acceptable for now.
- Round 2 move: keep the richer preface and avoid stripping it back down.

## Synthesis

Round 1 solved the humanity problem. Round 2 should solve the bridge problem. The site now looks and reads more like a student-facing learning product; the next refinement is making the guide pages feel even more connected to the live examples and experiences they describe.
