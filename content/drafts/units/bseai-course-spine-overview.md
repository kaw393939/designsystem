  - type: section
    id: artifact-ladder
    title: The ladder matters because it lets outsiders see growth without needing inside explanations.
    body: |
      The course spine should not promise that every course becomes public-facing in the same way. It should promise that every course contributes a visible capability surface.

      Early courses make the student's signal and first build legible. Middle courses deepen systems, requirements, and retrieval thinking. Later courses connect AI systems to evaluation, process change, and advanced depth. The result is not only a transcript. It is an increasingly legible record of what the student can do.

      The final slot remains selected advanced depth until leadership fixes the exact course. The public site should keep that placeholder honest rather than implying a settled promise.
